Flooding Mitigation in Wufang Village (2021~2023)

Wufang Village, a fishing community near the Donggang River, faces frequent flooding due to high tides and heavy rains. Its proximity to the shoreline worsens drainage conditions, especially during high tides, making the area vulnerable. Our team proposed solutions including pump stations, raised embankments, and automatic gates to prevent seawater inflow into fish ponds and city areas. My role involved simulating flood scenarios and analyzing potential solutions to guide decision-making. This experience reinforced my belief in the power of engineering to improve community resilience.

Flooding Mitigation with NBS (Natural Based Solutions)

Flooding has become a persistent issue in the area due to the inadequate height of the levees. Every time it rains, water overflows the levees and floods nearby villages. While the authorities have decided not to build higher levees because of the relatively small population and the perception that flood-related losses are limited, local residents continue to suffer significant disruptions. Additionally, farmlands in the region are frequently affected by flooding, leading to crop damage and financial losses for the farmers. After discussions with various parties, the final decision was to dredge the river and use farmland for flood detention. The original plan was to divert water from the canal into the farmland, but local farmers opposed the idea due to severe river pollution.In the end, only rainwater was retained in the farmland, which reduced the efficiency of the flood control measures.
